Top 10 Most Epic Battles in the JJK Manga

By Domic January 8, 2026

The JJK manga has given us some of the most memorable battles in modern shonen history. Gege Akutami's ability to craft fights that are both visually stunning and tactically deep sets this series apart. Here's our ranking of the top 10 most epic battles throughout the manga.

Note: This article contains spoilers for the entire manga series. Proceed with caution if you haven't finished reading.

10. Yuji & Nobara vs. Eso & Kechizu

This early battle showcased what makes JJK fights special. The coordination between Yuji and Nobara, combined with Nobara's willingness to harm herself to defeat her enemies, established the stakes and tone for future confrontations. The emotional weight of the brothers' bond added unexpected depth to what could have been a simple monster fight.

9. Maki's Zenin Clan Massacre

After the tragedy that befell her family, Maki's systematic destruction of the Zenin clan represented one of the most brutal sequences in the manga. Her complete mastery of combat without cursed energy proved that raw physical ability could match even the most powerful techniques. The panels depicting her rampage remain some of the most visceral in the series.

8. Yuji vs. Choso (Round 1)

This fight demonstrated how outclassed Yuji was against a truly dangerous opponent. Choso's Blood Manipulation technique pushed Yuji to his absolute limits, and the battle's conclusion—interrupted by Choso's sudden vision of Yuji as his brother—set up one of the manga's most unexpected character developments.

7. Gojo vs. Jogo

Our first real glimpse of Gojo's overwhelming power came in his confrontation with Jogo. The casual way Gojo dismantled a special-grade curse while explaining the mechanics of his Infinity and Domain Expansion established him as truly untouchable. This fight set the benchmark that other characters would be measured against.

6. Megumi vs. Reggie Star

The Culling Game gave Megumi a chance to shine, and his battle against Reggie demonstrated his tactical genius. Using the swimming pool environment and his Ten Shadows technique creatively, Megumi proved he belonged among the series' strongest fighters. The fight's pacing and strategic depth made it a standout.

5. Toji vs. Gojo (Hidden Inventory)

The flashback arc delivered one of the manga's most shocking moments when Toji Fushiguro actually killed Satoru Gojo. Watching the "strongest" fall to a man with zero cursed energy challenged everything we thought we knew about the power system. Gojo's subsequent resurrection and brutal counterattack only enhanced the impact.

4. Yuji & Todo vs. Mahito

The Shibuya Incident culminated in this incredible tag-team battle. Todo's Boogie Woogie combined with Yuji's Black Flash created one of the most dynamic fights in the series. The emotional stakes—avenging Nanami and Nobara—elevated every punch. Todo's sacrifice and Yuji's growth made this confrontation unforgettable.

3. Gojo vs. Sukuna

The battle fans waited years to see lived up to impossibly high expectations. Watching the two strongest beings in the series clash across multiple chapters, with both displaying the full extent of their abilities, was a masterclass in manga action. The outcome shocked readers and changed the trajectory of the entire story.

2. Shibuya Incident - All Battles

Considering Shibuya as a single extended battle sequence, nothing else in the manga matches its scope and impact. Multiple simultaneous confrontations, tragic deaths, shocking revelations, and the sealing of Gojo created an arc that redefined the series. Every character was tested, and the jujutsu world would never be the same.

1. The Final Showdown Against Sukuna

The manga's climactic battle brought together every surviving character against the fully-realized King of Curses. The relay-style combat, with different sorcerers taking turns against Sukuna, showcased the entire cast's growth. The emotional weight of previous losses, combined with the desperate hope of victory, made every chapter of this extended fight riveting.

Honorable Mentions

  • Nanami vs. Mahito
  • Yuta vs. Geto (Volume 0)
  • Sukuna vs. Mahoraga
  • Hakari vs. Kashimo
  • Gojo vs. Toji (Rematch)

Conclusion

The JJK manga's battles succeed because they combine spectacular action with meaningful character development and consequences. Each fight advances the plot and reveals something new about the participants. This commitment to meaningful combat is why these battles resonate so strongly with readers.
